Support

Help and guidance for using OncsCare

Emergency Situations

If you are experiencing severe symptoms or a medical emergency, do not rely on the app. Contact your clinical team immediately or call 999 for emergency services.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I use the OncsCare app?
OncsCare is designed to be simple and intuitive. Log your daily symptoms by opening the app and following the prompts. Your clinical team will review your entries to support your care.
What if I miss a day of logging?
Don't worry if you occasionally miss logging your symptoms. Simply continue with your regular logging schedule. Consistent use helps your clinical team better understand your condition.
Is my data secure?
Yes, your data is encrypted and stored on NHS-approved secure servers. We follow strict NHS Digital data protection standards and UK GDPR compliance.
Can I withdraw from the study?
Yes, you can withdraw from the research study at any time without affecting your clinical care. Contact the research team using the details provided in the Contact section.
Who can see my symptom data?
Only your authorized clinical team members and approved NHS research staff can access your data, and only when necessary for your care or the research study.

Technical Support

If you experience technical issues with the app:

  • Try closing and reopening the app
  • Ensure you have a stable internet connection
  • Check that your device meets the minimum requirements
  • Contact the research team if problems persist

App Requirements

  • iOS 12.0 or later / Android 8.0 or later
  • Stable internet connection for data synchronization
  • At least 50MB of available storage space

Using the App Effectively

Best Practices

  • Log symptoms at the same time each day for consistency
  • Be honest and accurate in your symptom reporting
  • Contact your clinical team if you have concerns about severe symptoms
  • Keep the app updated to the latest version

Understanding Triage Levels

The app uses a triage system to help prioritize care:

  • Green: Symptoms are within expected ranges
  • Yellow: Symptoms require monitoring
  • Red: Symptoms require immediate clinical attention

Note: The triage system is a tool to support clinical decision-making but does not replace clinical judgment or emergency protocols.

Getting Additional Help

For questions not covered in this support section:

  • Review the Privacy Policy for data-related questions
  • Use the Contact page to reach the research team
  • Speak with your clinical team during appointments
  • Contact your usual hospital helpline for urgent clinical concerns

Response Times

We aim to respond to support inquiries within 2-3 business days. For urgent clinical matters, please contact your clinical team directly using your usual hospital contact methods.
